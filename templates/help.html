How To

How to titrate one acid with a base?

1. Add the volume of your sample in liter
2. Add a concentration of your sample in mol/L
3. Add the pKs value(s) of your acidic sample. Your acid may have several pKs values so you have to enter them in square brackets e.g [2.16, 7.21, 12.32] for phosphoric acid or [-6] for hydrochloric acid.
4. Set the base volume and concentration to zero and enter [0] for the pKs values.
5. Enter your total volume (liter). If you titrate your acidic sample without dilution you can enter the same volume as you entered as sample volume (step 1). If your acidic sample is diluted with water, enter the total volume after dilution.
6. Add the concentration of your titrant (base to titrate your acidic sample)
7. Add the volume of your titrant
8. Add the pKs value(s) of your titrant e.g. [14] for sodium hydroxide or [9.25] for ammonia. Here, the pKs values have also to be in square brackets
9. Click Compute button to simulate the titration

How to titrate a base with an acid?

1. Set the volume and the concentration of the acid zero and enter [0] as the pKs value of the acid.
2. Add the volume of your alkaline sample in liter
3. Add the conentration of your alkaline sample in mol/L
4. Add the pKs value(s) of your base. Your base may have several pKs values so you have to enter them in square brackets e.g. [2.16, 7.21, 12.32] for Tripotassium phosphate or [9.25] for ammonia.
5. Follow the steps 5 to 9 above.

How to titrate multiple acids with a base?

1. Add your acid volumes comma seperated in liter e.g. 0.02, 0.015 (20 ml acid A, 15 ml acid B)
2. Add your acid concentrations comma seperated in mol/L e.g. 0.1, 0.05 (acid A = 0.1 M, acid B = 0.05 M)
3. Add your pKs values in comma sepearted square brackets e.g. [-6],[2.16, 7.21, 12.32] acid A = HCl, acid B = H3PO4. Your acids may have as many pKs values as you need. 4. Follow the steps 4 to 9 above.

How to titrate a mixture of acids and bases?

It works like the titration of multiple acids combined with the titration of multiple bases. You should know wheter your sample mixture is acidic or alkaline. So you can choose the right titrant. It might be, that you get useless results, if you titrate an acid with an acid for example.
